Periodic Conferences
Through periodic conferences and workshops, the NFP twinning process is currently being piloted in the African Region.

Potential twinning partner countries were identified based on SPAR and JEE scores, expressed needs, country profiles, and preferred language of communication. Rwanda, Uganda, Nigeria and South Africa were identified through the initial selection process.
IHR Knowledge Networks
Guided by the second pillar of the five-year global strategic plan for public health preparedness and response (2018-2023), WHO launched the Global Knowledge Network of IHR NFPs in 2018, with the aim of strengthening IHR NFPs capacities to effectively perform their functions through knowledge creation and knowledge transfer.

WHO has supported the establishment of several regional NFP Knowledge Networks since 2018, including the AFRO IHR NFP KN, launched in August 2018; the EURO NFP KN, launched in October 2018; the SEAR NFP+ KN, launched in March 2019; the PAHO NFP KN, established in October 2019; and the EMRO NFP KN, which was discussed in March 2022.

Twinning Project
In support of the continuous development and learning of NFPs, WHO is developing an IHR NFP Twinning programme that seeks to strengthen NFPs capacities to fulfil their mandatory functions through a cross-cultural, reciprocal process where NFP teams from two different countries will work together to complementarily address their IHR implementation gaps. The NFP Twinning program provides a learning experience tailored to each NFP team’s needs and areas of expertise as it focuses on partnership-based approach.

Two modalities have been identified by WHO for NFP Twinning:
  • Mutual Twinning where one NFP who is strong in a specific technical area supports another NFP in that same area and vice versa; Knowledge sharing and training in areas in which NFPs’ knowledge and expertise are complementary.
  • Thematic Twinning where NFPs with similar gaps work together in co-learning, knowledge and experiences sharing in the same technical area.
Once the NFP Twinning Platform is launched, NFPs can search for and communicate with potential twinning partners online.
