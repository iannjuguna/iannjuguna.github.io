IHR Learning Program
The IHR Learning Programme is a collection of resources targeted for NFP and IHR stakeholders’ capacity building toward improved IHR implementation at the country level.

The programme responds to an identified limitation among NFPs and IHR stakeholders in knowledge and awareness of existing training and learning tools on IHR.
IHR Catalogue
WHO releases bi-annually the Health Security and International Health Regulations Training Catalogue to provide IHR stakeholders with available learning resources, available on the Health Security Learning Platform.

IHR Learning Country Package (IHR LCP)
To further support IHR implementation at the country level, WHO aims to develop an IHR Learning Country Package with the objective of providing key IHR learning resources to build and strengthen the capacity of national IHR stakeholders in general and in special contexts including in Fragile, Conflict and Violence (FCV) and Small Island Developing States (SIDS) settings. The design of the IHR Learning Country Package ensures that country can adapt the resources and hence promote ownership and self-implementation.

The country package contains the following components:
  • A catalogue of available IHR learning resources
  • IHR Competency Framework
  • Recommended learning pathways for different stakeholders
  • An IHR Learning Country Package implementation guide
  • Advocacy/communication plan
  • Evaluation framework and tools
NFP Onboarding Package
The NFP Onboarding package is at strengthening the capacities of NFPs through regular and targeted trainings and workshops both at national and regional levels.

More specifically within the first three months of their appointment, the NFP onboarding program will strengthen the knowledge and skills of newly appointed NFP representatives and provide them with tools to facilitate the effective delivery of their NFP functions. The NFP onboarding package will also support more advanced future IHR learning.
EPIC Game
EPIC is a serious game that provides an immersive learning experience in which participants can practice in a safe learning environment.

Participants take on different roles in a multi-sectoral task force and make key decisions on the best course of actions to take to mitigate an emerging public health threat - with limited resources.

The target audience for EPIC Game training include IHR NFPs, IHR stakeholders, senior government officials, and academia; plus everyone who practice public health, IHR provision, IHR capacity building, and health emergency preparedness & readiness for health security.
