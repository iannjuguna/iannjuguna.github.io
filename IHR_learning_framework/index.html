About the IHR Learning Framework
The Review Committee on Second Extensions for Establishing National Public Health Capacities and on IHR Implementation, Review Committee on the Functioning of IHR during the COVID-19 response and the Independent Oversight and Advisory Committee (IOAC) all strongly recommended the strengthening of the National Focal Points (NFPs) for the IHR. In particular, the April 2021 IHR Review Committee report stipulated that “WHO should continue working with States Parties to strengthen capacities of NFPs, including through regular and targeted training and workshops,especially at national and regional levels."

This recommendation was in response to the Committee’s identification of existing gaps within the NFPs, particularly the lack of authority, low capacity, limited training, and resources needed for their optimal functionality. More specifically, the Committee’s recommendations tasked for the critical provision of national legal frameworks to ensure IHR implementation and public health control measures; mechanisms for government-wide accountability; strengthening risk assessments, monitoring, data reporting and information sharing; and the empowerment of the role of National Focal Points.

Closely linked to the much-needed response to the Review Committee’s recommendations is the Learning Solutions and Training Unit’s (LST) mandate to provide learning resources and facilitate knowledge building, peer-to-peer learning and experience sharing for NFPs and IHR stakeholders. To guide this, LST drafted the IHR Learning Framework, which pillars are designed to support in-service workforce development and learning, aimed at strengthening the functions of National IHR Focal Points and IHR stakeholders.

The IHR Learning Framework is comprised of the following 4 components:
IHR Learning Programme
IHR Competency Framework
IHR Knowledge Networks
IHR Digital Learning Environment
IHR Learning Program
The IHR Learning Programme responds to an identified limitation among NFPs and IHR stakeholders in knowledge and awareness of existing training and learning tools on IHR.
It is therefore a collection of resources targeted for NFP and IHR stakeholders’ capacity building toward improved IHR implementation at the country level.
NFP Onboarding Package
The NFP Onboarding package aims to strengthen the capacities of NFPs through regular and targeted trainings and workshops both at national and regional levels.

The program aims to strengthen the knowledge and skills of newly appointed NFP representatives and provide them with tools to facilitate the effective delivery of their NFP functions.

Country Package
A set of IHR learning resources that countries can select and adapt according to their country context and needs.
EPIC Game
EPIC is a serious game training project that seeks to strengthen IHR stakeholders and NFPs competencies.
IHR Competency Framework
WHO and the IDEALS Institute at Johns Hopkins University (JHU) School of Education are collaborating on the creation of a learner-centered and outcomes-based competency model.
The model empowers IHR Stakeholders with the knowledge, skills and behaviours required to implement the IHR.
IHR Stakeholders Competency Framework
This process of developing competency frameworks includes a comprehensive review of all IHR competencies through interviews and focus groups with relevant stakeholders.

National IHR Focal Points Competency Framework
The draft NFP Competency Framework (NFP CF) matrix contains 142 behavioral indicators for mandatory and supplementary NFP functions distributed across 19 domains and five competency groupings.

Learning Pathways
To develop learning pathways, identification of the workforce and the expected intended formal workforce credentials is required.
Skills and Indicators
The indicator measures the number of learners that have achieved a pre-defined minimum learning outcomes in a given skills-based training program.
Certification & Credentials Model
Certification and Credential model details are being developed.
IHR Knowledge Networks
The knowledge network seeks to bring together NFP professionals and associated stakeholders, including actors in the humanitarian sector the Global Health Cluster, to collaborate and learn from one another.
This will bolster international cooperation and better support IHR implementation and operational about readiness.
IHR Knowledge Networks
WHO aims to strengthen IHR NFPs capacities to effectively perform their functions through knowledge creation and knowledge transfer.

NFP Knowledge Networks
The NFP Knowledge Network will be a digital space for publicly sharing updates on NFP-related IHR topics, lessons learned and emerging practices.

Twinning Project
Twinning Project is a cross-cultural, reciprocal learning scape where NFP teams from two different countries will work together to complementarily address their IHR implementation gaps.
Periodic Conferences
Through periodic conferences and workshops, the NFP twinning process is currently being piloted in the African Region.
IHR Digital Learning Environment
The IHR digital learning environment (DLE) seeks to provide one-stop full NFP access to a wide range of online digital learning solutions such as the digital competency framework and associated learning pathways.
The IHR learning programme (the digital catalogue), the Serious Game (EPIC) complementary microlearning training resources, as well as web-based learning applications (Game Learning/Simulations, Twinning and Knowledge Networks), will also be available on the platform.

Monitoring Dashboards
DLE dashboards are specifically designed to provide timely information for each participant on the knowledge and skills they have acquired, as well as a record of the trainings they have attended.
