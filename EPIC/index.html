What is EPIC Game?
EPIC is a serious game that provides an immersive learning experience in which participants can practice in a safe learning environment.
Participants take on different roles in a multi-sectoral task force and make key decisions on the best course of actions to take to mitigate an emerging public health threat - with limited resources.
The target audience for EPIC Game training include IHR NFPs, IHR stakeholders, senior government officials, and academia; plus everyone who practice public health, IHR provision, IHR capacity building, and health emergency preparedness & readiness for health security.
Game Objectives
Priority setting
Define priority activities and allocate resources to strengthen capacities.
Cooperation
Foster cooperation among all key sectors despite diverging interests.
Coordination
Coordinate with relevant stakeholders at local, national, regional and international levels.
Communication
Communicate early and effectively on the Public Health events to relevant stakeholders.

Frequently Asked Questions

Below are some of the frequently asked questions. Click on each question to view the response and learn more about the EPIC game.

What kind of device and resources do I need to play the game?

• EPIC is a web application and it runs on a browser like Firefox or Chrome. For the best view, you need a computer or a laptop with internet access.

Can I enroll my whole team/department?

• You can submit your list of interested participants to us via email and from there we can organize for an EPIC session for your team.

Do I get any form of certification once I play the game?

• While currently this feature is not available, it is under consideration.

How many sessions of the game can an individual play?

• A typical game session last for about 1.5 hrs. You can play one session of the game per registration but you can always register for another game session.

Is EPIC a single or a multi-player game?

• While you can play EPIC in single-player mode, you stand to gain more from the diverse views and complexity of decision making brought on by multiple players.
